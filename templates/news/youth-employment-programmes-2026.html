Youth Employment Programmes in South Africa 2026

Published: 16 February 2026 7 min read By SA Daily Portal News, Employment

Following the State of the Nation Address, President Ramaphosa released his weekly newsletter on 16 February 2026 detailing government's youth employment initiatives. This article summarizes information from official government sources.

Key Statistics at a Glance

  • BEEI work opportunities created: More than 1.3 million
  • Schools with education assistants: 19,000 no-fee primary schools
  • Education assistants with tertiary qualifications: 32%
  • ECD children reached: Over 50,000

üìö Basic Education Employment Initiative (BEEI)

The Basic Education Employment Initiative was founded in 2020 as part of the Presidential Employment Stimulus. It deploys young people to schools as education and general school assistants.

  • Has created more than 1.3 million work opportunities to date.
  • It is the largest youth employment programme in South Africa's history.
  • Education assistants placed in 19,000 no-fee primary schools.
  • Assistants support numeracy and serve as Reading Champions to improve literacy.
  • "The work of the education assistants allows teachers to spend more time on teaching and on lesson preparation" ‚Äì President Ramaphosa.

‚úÖ Programme Requirements

  • General school assistants: Need to have at least a Grade 9 qualification.
  • Education assistants: Need at least a matric certificate.
  • 32% of education assistants had some form of tertiary qualification.
  • 14% held a teaching qualification.
  • Training provided includes: school safety, online safety, financial literacy, word processing, AI fluency and coding.

üìä Presidential Youth Employment Intervention (PYEI) Results

  • PYEI has reached more than 5 million young people since inception.
  • 2.36 million earning opportunities facilitated through SA Youth.

Third quarter results (Oct-Dec 2025):

  • 294,530 earning opportunities secured for youth.
  • More than 11,000 work experience opportunities created through partnerships.
  • 132,784 young people placed in paid service opportunities through the National Youth Service.

üß∏ Early Childhood Development Support

  • More than 1,000 previously disadvantaged ECD centres receiving support.
  • Centres receive nutritional support, toys, books and learning materials.
  • The Social Employment Fund now reaches over 50,000 children in ECD centres across the country.

President Ramaphosa stated in his newsletter:

"Meeting the constitutional imperative to provide quality education to our nation's young is an all-of-society effort. These initiatives illustrate clearly the benefits of multisectoral cooperation between government, the private sector and civil society.
