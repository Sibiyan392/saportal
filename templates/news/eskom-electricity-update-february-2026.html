Following President Ramaphosa's State of the Nation Address on 12 February 2026, Eskom has released updated performance data. This article summarizes information from Eskom and official government sources.

February 2026 Snapshot

  • Energy Availability Factor (EAF): 65.04% (year to date)
  • Unplanned outages: Average 11,397 MW
  • Transmission reform: Independent state-owned operator confirmed

üè≠ Independent Transmission System Operator Confirmed

In his SONA 2026 address, President Ramaphosa directed that a fully independent state-owned transmission entity will be established, with ownership and control of transmission assets and responsibility for operating the electricity market.

  • The new entity will be completely separated from Eskom.
  • A dedicated task team under the National Energy Crisis Committee will report within three months.
  • Approximately R390 billion needed over the next decade to upgrade the transmission network.

üìä Energy Availability Factor Reaches 65.04%

Eskom reports that for the financial year to date (1 April 2025 to 12 February 2026), the Energy Availability Factor has increased to 65.04%.

  • The generation fleet has achieved or exceeded the 70% mark on 66 occasions during this period.
  • Between 6 and 12 February 2026, average unplanned outages measured 11,397 MW.
  • Unplanned Capacity Loss Factor stood at 23.29%.
  • Planned maintenance: 15.79% over same period.

‚õΩ Diesel Spend Reduced

Improved plant performance has significantly reduced reliance on diesel-fired generation.

  • Year to date diesel expenditure: R4.88 billion lower than same period last year.
  • Diesel usage over the past week was minimal and largely associated with test runs.
  • 3,181 MW of capacity currently in cold reserve due to excess supply.

‚ö° Grid Stability and Reserve Margin

Eskom reports that the national power system remains stable, supported by improved plant performance and continued implementation of the Generation Recovery Plan.

Monday 16 February 2026 forecast:

  • Evening peak demand forecast: 23,542 MW.
  • Available capacity projected: 27,350 MW.
  • Eskom will bring 2,429 MW of generation capacity online ahead of the evening peak.

‚òÄÔ∏è Renewable Energy Target: 40% by 2030

Government has set a target for more than 40% of South Africa's energy supply to come from renewable sources by 2030.

  • Regulatory reforms have unlocked significant private sector investment in wind and solar.
  • Private renewable energy investments have already exceeded R200 billion and added around 6,000 MW of capacity.
  • R250 billion in international pledges under Just Energy Transition Investment Plan.

üöó New Energy Vehicle Incentives

From March 2026, government will introduce a 150% tax deduction for investment in new energy vehicles, alongside support measures for local battery manufacturing.
