SONA 2026: Key Announcements That Affect South Africans

Published: 13 February 2026 8 min read By SA Daily Portal News, Politics

President Cyril Ramaphosa delivered the 2026 State of the Nation Address (SONA) on Thursday 12 February 2026 at Cape Town City Hall. Political analyst Professor Sam Koma described it as "arguably the most action-oriented speech" of Ramaphosa's nine years in office. Here is a factual summary of the key announcements.

SONA 2026 at a Glance

  • Date: 12 February 2026
  • Location: Cape Town City Hall
  • Theme: Safety, growth and rebuilding the state

üëÆ SANDF Deployment to Gang Hotspots

President Ramaphosa described organised crime as "the most immediate threat to our democracy, our society and our economic development."

Key security announcements:

  • SANDF deployment: The President confirmed he is deploying the South African National Defence Force to support police operations in the Western Cape and Gauteng to deal with gang violence and illegal mining.
  • 5,500 new police officers: Government will recruit an additional 5,500 police officers this year, adding to the 20,000 previously announced.
  • Gun crime measures: Streamlining legislation on licensing, possessing and trading firearms, with increased enforcement of existing gun laws.
  • Re-vetting of SAPS and metro police senior management.
  • Lifestyle audits for law enforcement officials.
  • Whistle-Blower Protection Bill to be introduced.

üíß National Water Crisis Committee Established

Water was described by the President as "the single most important issue for many people in South Africa."

  • National Water Crisis Committee: Established and will be chaired personally by the President.
  • R156 billion investment: Government has committed more than R156 billion over the next three years for water and sanitation infrastructure.
  • R54 billion incentive for metros to reform utilities.
  • Municipal accountability: Criminal charges have already been laid against 56 municipalities for failing to meet their obligations.

Target sectors include: Energy, Water, Transport, and Digital infrastructure.

Specialised commercial courts will be established to fast-track economic disputes affecting infrastructure projects.

‚ö° Energy Reforms and Independent Transmission Entity

Having declared load shedding over, government is shifting focus to long-term energy reform.

  • Independent transmission entity: A fully independent state-owned transmission company will own and control transmission assets and operate the electricity market.
  • Renewable energy target: By 2030, more than 40% of energy supply is expected to come from renewable sources.
  • R250 billion JET funding: International pledges under South Africa's Just Energy Transition Investment Plan now total approximately R250 billion.

The President addressed the outbreak affecting the livestock sector, described as "one of the worst outbreaks of foot-and-mouth disease our country has experienced."

  • R28 million allocated for foot-and-mouth disease vaccinations.
  • National herd vaccination: 14 million cattle requiring 28 million vaccines over the next 12 months.
  • Task team established of farmer organisations and experts, reporting monthly to the President.
