NSFAS Application Guide 2026: How to Apply for University Funding

Updated: February 2026 12 min read By Nompumelelo

The National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) provides bursaries to South African students from low-income households who want to study at public universities and TVET colleges. For millions of students, NSFAS funding is the key that unlocks access to higher education and a better future.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about applying for NSFAS in 2026. We'll walk you through eligibility requirements, required documents, the step-by-step application process, how to check your status, and what to do if your application is unsuccessful.

NSFAS Key Facts 2026

  • Application period: Typically September - January
  • Funding covers: Tuition, registration, accommodation, transport, living allowance
  • Household income threshold: R350,000 per year or less
  • Application fee: FREE ‚Äì NSFAS does not charge to apply

1. What is NSFAS?

The National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) is a public entity that provides financial assistance to eligible students who wish to study at South Africa's 26 public universities and 50 TVET colleges.

What NSFAS Covers

  • Tuition and registration fees ‚Äì Paid directly to the institution
  • Accommodation ‚Äì University residences or accredited private accommodation
  • Transport ‚Äì For students who commute
  • Living allowance ‚Äì For food and personal expenses
  • Book allowance ‚Äì For learning materials

How NSFAS Works

  • It's a bursary (not a loan) ‚Äì you don't have to pay it back if you meet academic requirements
  • Funding is renewed each year based on academic performance
  • NSFAS uses a means test to determine eligibility based on household income

2. NSFAS Eligibility Criteria 2026

Who Can Apply?

  • South African citizens (permanent residents and refugees may also qualify)
  • Household income: Combined annual household income must be R350,000 or less
  • Academic requirements: Must have applied for admission to a public university or TVET college
  • SASSA grant recipients: Automatically qualify (no means test needed)
  • Students with disabilities: No income threshold ‚Äì all eligible regardless of household income

Who Does NOT Qualify?

  • Students who already have an undergraduate degree (except for students with disabilities)
  • Students studying at private institutions (NSFAS only funds public institutions)
  • Students whose household income exceeds R350,000 per year

4. How to Apply for NSFAS Online

1

Create a myNSFAS Account

Go to www.nsfas.org.za and click on "myNSFAS" then "Register". Use your own ID number, cellphone number, and email address.

2

Log In and Start Application

Log in to your myNSFAS account and click on "Apply" (only visible during application period).

3

Complete Personal Information

Fill in your contact details, address, and indicate if you have a disability.

4

Provide Family/Household Information

Add details of parents/guardians and siblings. This information is used for the means test.

5

Upload Required Documents

Upload all certified documents. Ensure files are clear and under 2MB each.

6

Review and Submit

Review all information for accuracy, check the declaration box, and click "Submit". You'll receive a confirmation SMS and email with your reference number.

NEVER Pay to Apply

NSFAS applications are completely FREE. If anyone asks you for money to help with your application, it's a scam. Report such incidents to NSFAS.

5. How to Check Your NSFAS Application Status

Step-by-Step Status Check

  1. Go to www.nsfas.org.za
  2. Click on "myNSFAS" and log in with your ID number and password
  3. Your dashboard will show your application status

Understanding NSFAS Statuses

Application Received

Your application has been submitted successfully and is in the queue.

Validation

Your information is being verified with external sources (SARS, Home Affairs).

Funding Eligible

Congratulations! You've been approved for funding.

Application Unsuccessful

Your application did not meet the criteria. Check the reason and consider appealing.

6. How to Appeal an Unsuccessful Application

Appeal Process

  1. Log in to your myNSFAS account
  2. Click on "Track Funding Progress"
  3. Select "Application Unsuccessful" and click "Appeal"
  4. Select the reason for your appeal and upload supporting documents
  5. Submit your appeal within 30 days of receiving your status

Valid Reasons to Appeal

  • Income threshold: If your family income has decreased
  • Incorrect information: If your application contained errors
  • Missing documents: If you forgot to upload something
  • SASSA status: If you're a SASSA beneficiary but it wasn't reflected

Frequently Asked Questions

When do NSFAS applications open for 2026?
Applications typically open in September 2025 and close in January 2026. Check the official NSFAS website for exact dates.
What is the income threshold for NSFAS?
The combined annual household income must be R350,000 or less. For students with disabilities, there is no income threshold.
Can I apply if I'm already studying?
Yes, current university and TVET college students can apply for NSFAS funding. You'll need to provide your latest academic results.
Do I have to pay NSFAS back?
No, NSFAS funding is a bursary, not a loan. You do not need to repay it provided you meet the academic requirements.
I'm a SASSA grant recipient. Do I need to provide proof of income?
SASSA grant recipients are automatically financially eligible. Provide your SASSA letter or bank statement showing grant payments.
