How to Start an Online Business in South Africa (2026)

Updated: February 2026 15 min read By Nompumelelo

South Africa's digital economy is booming. With over 43 million internet users and e-commerce sales growing at 25% annually, there's never been a better time to start an online business. This guide walks you through every step.

E-commerce Statistics (2026)

  • Internet users: 43.5 million
  • Online shoppers: 24 million+
  • E-commerce growth: 25% year-on-year

1. Choose Your Business Model

E-commerce Store

Sell physical products. Hold inventory and ship to customers.

Startup: R5,000 - R50,000+

Dropshipping

Sell without inventory. Supplier ships directly.

Startup: R2,000 - R10,000

Digital Products

Sell e-books, courses, templates, software.

Startup: R0 - R5,000

Service Business

Offer consulting, coaching, design, writing.

Startup: R0 - R2,000

5. Legal Requirements

Required Policies on Your Website

  • Privacy Policy: POPIA compliant
  • Terms and Conditions: Rules for using your site
  • Returns Policy: CPA requires 7-day return period
  • Shipping Policy: Delivery times and costs

Consumer Protection Act (CPA)

Customers have the right to return goods within 7 days for any reason. You can charge a reasonable restocking fee.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to start an online business?
You can start with as little as R500-1,000 for a basic website and initial marketing. Costs vary by model: service business (R0-500), digital products (R0-2,000), dropshipping (R2,000-5,000), e-commerce (R5,000-50,000+).
Do I need to register my business?
You can start as a sole proprietor without formal registration. Register a Pty Ltd when you're generating consistent income for limited liability and professional image.
What's the best platform for an online store?
Shopify is easiest for beginners. WooCommerce gives more control. Takealot Marketplace gives immediate access to SA's largest customer base.
How do I accept payments?
Use PayFast (easiest, no monthly fees), Ozow (instant EFT), or Yoco (online + card machines). Most e-commerce platforms integrate with these.
