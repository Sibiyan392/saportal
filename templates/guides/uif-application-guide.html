UIF Application Guide 2026

Complete step-by-step guide to claim unemployment benefits from the Unemployment Insurance Fund

8 min read Updated March 2026
Nompumelelo Sibiya March 2026 UIF Guide
What is UIF? The Unemployment Insurance Fund provides short-term financial relief to workers who become unemployed, or to contributors who are unable to work due to maternity, illness, or adoption.

Who Can Claim UIF?

You qualify if:

  • You were contributing to UIF while employed
  • You lost your job through no fault of your own (retrenched, dismissed, contract ended)
  • You are on maternity leave or sick leave
  • You are an adoptive parent
  • You applied within 6 months of becoming unemployed
⚠️ Note: You cannot claim if you resigned voluntarily or were fired for misconduct.

Required Documents for UIF Application

Prepare these documents before applying:

  • ID document (Smart ID or green ID book) - original and copy
  • UI-19 form (completed by your employer)
  • UI-2.7 form (application form)
  • Bank account details (confirmation letter from bank)
  • Letter of termination/retrenchment from employer
  • Last 6 months payslips
  • Proof of residence (municipal bill or affidavit)
  • Police affidavit if documents are lost

Step-by-Step UIF Application Process

Step 1: Get UI-19 Form from Employer
Your employer MUST complete the UI-19 form (Declaration of Employment). This form confirms your employment period, salary, reason for termination, and UIF contributions made.
Step 2: Complete UI-2.7 Application Form
Download from labour.gov.za or get from labour centre. Fill in your personal details accurately, provide banking information, sign and date the form, and attach all supporting documents.
Step 3: Submit at Labour Centre
Visit your nearest Department of Employment and Labour office. Bring all original documents and certified copies, take a number, submit application, and receive a receipt or reference number.
Step 4: Online Application (uFiling)
Register at ufiling.labour.gov.za, create an account with your ID and email, complete your profile, upload all required documents, submit application online, and track status through your dashboard.
Step 5: Wait for Processing
Processing takes 4-8 weeks. You'll receive SMS updates. Payments are made directly to your bank account every 2 weeks for up to 34 weeks.

How UIF Benefits Are Calculated

Your UIF benefit is based on your previous salary and how long you contributed:

  • First 238 days (about 34 weeks): 38% of your previous salary (up to a maximum)
  • Remaining days (if applicable): 30% of previous salary
  • Maximum benefit: R17,712 per month (for 2026)
  • Minimum benefit: You must have contributed for at least 13 weeks
📊 Example: If your salary was R8,000 per month, your UIF benefit would be approximately R3,040 per month (38%).

UIF Payment Schedule

Once approved, payments are made every 14 days for up to 238 days (about 8 months) per unemployment period. Funds go directly into your account.

After Approval:
• Sign the unemployment register monthly at the labour centre
• Declare if you find new employment
• Payments stop automatically when you start new job
• You can claim again if you become unemployed later

Common UIF Problems and Solutions

Problem: Employer hasn't registered me or paid contributions
Solution: Lodge a complaint at the labour centre. The employer can be fined.

Problem: UI-19 form missing or incorrect
Solution: Contact your employer immediately. If they refuse, report to labour centre.

Problem: Application taking too long
Solution: Check status at uFiling or visit labour centre with your reference number.

Problem: Payment not received
Solution: Verify bank details are correct. If payments stop, re-submit UI-2.7 form.

Important Deadlines:
• Apply within 6 months of losing your job
• Sign unemployment register monthly to keep receiving benefits
• Report new employment immediately to avoid overpayment claims

UIF for Maternity, Illness, and Adoption

Maternity Benefits: 17 weeks (121 days) at 66% of salary. Apply 4 weeks before due date.

Illness Benefits: Up to 34 weeks if unable to work due to illness. Doctor's certificate required.

Adoption Benefits: 10 weeks (if child under 2 years) at 66% of salary.

UIF Contact Information:
UIF Call Centre: 0800 030 007 (toll-free)
Website: www.labour.gov.za
uFiling: ufiling.labour.gov.za
WhatsApp: 082 046 8553
Beware of Scams:
• UIF will NEVER ask for your banking PIN or password
• Only apply through official channels
• Never pay anyone to process your UIF claim
• Report fraud to the UIF hotline
