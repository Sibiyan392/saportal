How to Earn Money as a Student in South Africa (2026)

Updated: February 2026 12 min read By Nompumelelo

Being a student in South Africa comes with significant financial challenges. Between tuition fees, textbooks, accommodation, transport, data, and daily expenses, the costs add up quickly. While many students rely on NSFAS or family support, having your own income gives you independence and reduces financial stress.

The good news is that there are more opportunities than ever for students to earn money while studying. From flexible online work to part-time jobs that work around your lecture schedule, this guide covers all the practical ways South African students can earn income in 2026 without sacrificing their grades.

Student Earning Facts 2026

  • Average student earns: R1,500 - R4,000/month part-time
  • Most flexible options: Online surveys, freelance writing, tutoring
  • Top students earn: R8,000+/month through freelancing
  • Ideal hours: 10-15 hours per week is best for most students

1. Online Opportunities (Most Flexible)

Work from your residence or anywhere with internet, at hours that suit your schedule.

Online Surveys

R500-1,500/mo 5-10 hrs/wk Easy

Perfect for downtime between lectures. Sign up for multiple SA panels:

  • OpinionWorld – R100 EFT/vouchers
  • SurveyCompare – Cash via EFT
  • i-Say – Vouchers

Freelance Writing

R1,500-5,000/mo 8-12 hrs/wk Medium

If you're good at writing, this pays well. Start on:

  • Fiverr – Create writing gigs
  • Upwork – Bid on writing jobs
  • iWriter – Content mill (lower pay, easier)

Transcription

R1,000-3,500/mo 10-15 hrs/wk Medium

Type audio/video to text. Need 60+ WPM typing speed.

  • Rev – Largest platform
  • GoTranscript – Easier to start
  • TranscribeMe – Short audio clips

Micro-Tasks

R500-2,000/mo 5-10 hrs/wk Easy

Small tasks that require no special skills.

  • Clickworker – Data entry, research
  • Remotasks – AI training
  • Appen – Flexible projects

2. On-Campus Jobs

Convenient, no travel costs, and understanding of student schedules.

Library Assistant

R40-60/hr Flexible

Quiet work environment. Can study during slow periods. Apply at your university library.

IT Helpdesk

R50-80/hr Flexible

Help students with computer/network issues. Good if you're tech-savvy.

Campus Cafeteria

R30-50/hr Meal times

Food service jobs. Often includes free meals during shifts.

Student Ambassador

R50-80/hr Events

Represent university at open days and events. Good for CV building.

How to Find On-Campus Jobs

  • Check your university's student affairs/careers portal
  • Visit notice boards around campus
  • Ask lecturers or department administrators
  • Join university social media groups
  • Apply early – jobs fill quickly at start of semester

3. Tutoring – Turn Your Knowledge into Cash

If you're strong in certain subjects, tutoring is a natural fit for students.

School Subjects (Matric)

R100-200/hr

Tutor high school learners in Maths, Science, English, etc.

Platforms: Teach Me 2, SchoolAhead, local Facebook groups

First-Year University

R150-250/hr

Tutor first-year students in courses you did well in. Very high demand.

Advertise in first-year Facebook groups, offer group sessions.

Online Tutoring

$10-30/hr

Tutor international students online.

Platforms: Preply, Cambly (English teaching)

Tutoring Tips

  • Start with subjects you got 70%+ in
  • Offer first session free to attract clients
  • Group sessions (2-3 students) = more money per hour
  • Use library or study venues for sessions

4. Retail & Hospitality (Part-Time)

Retail Assistant

R25-40/hr

Work at shopping centres near campus. Evenings and weekends.

Companies: Checkers, Shoprite, Woolworths, Mr Price, Clicks

Waiter / Waitron

R20-40/hr + tips

Restaurants near campus. Tips can double your earnings.

Movie Theatre

R25-35/hr

Ster-Kinekor or Nu Metro. Perks include free movies.

Time Management Warning

Retail jobs often require fixed shifts. Make sure they don't clash with lectures and exams. Communicate your timetable clearly when applying.

5. Use Your Creative Skills

Graphic Design (Canva)

R100-300/hr

Create social media graphics for small businesses. Learn Canva in a weekend.

Photography

R300-800/session

Offer graduation photos, events, portraits. Build Instagram portfolio.

Video Editing

R200-500/video

Edit videos for YouTubers, content creators. Learn DaVinci Resolve or CapCut.

Student Success Story

"I started tutoring first-year Economics students in my second year. I charged R150/hour and had 5 regular clients. That was R3,000 extra per month. By third year, I expanded to group sessions and made R5,000/month while maintaining my distinctions."

- Amanda, UCT student

6. How to Balance Work and Studies

The key is finding the right balance so your grades don't suffer.

Limit Hours

10-15 hours per week is ideal. More than 20 hours often affects grades.

Plan Ahead

Reduce work during exams and assignment deadlines.

Choose Flexible Work

Online work lets you choose your hours.

Don't Skip Sleep

Your health and studies come first.

7. Important: NSFAS and Earnings

NSFAS Rules

If you receive NSFAS funding, check the current rules about earning while studying. Typically, students may earn up to R2,500 per month without affecting funding, but rules can change. Always verify with your financial aid office.

  • Keep records of all earnings
  • Declare income if required by NSFAS
  • Don't let work affect academic progress
  • Ask your financial aid office for clarity

Frequently Asked Questions

I'm a first-year student with no skills. Where should I start?

Start with online surveys – they require no skills and you can start immediately. While doing surveys, identify one skill to develop (Canva design, writing, tutoring in your best subject). Take free online courses (Google Digital Skills, Coursera). Within 2-3 months, you can start freelancing.

How many hours should I work as a student?

10-15 hours per week is ideal for most students. This typically earns R1,500 - R3,000 per month. During holidays, you can increase to 20-30 hours. Always prioritize your studies – education is your long-term investment.

What's the fastest way to make money as a student?

For immediate cash (within days): sign up for multiple survey sites (OpinionWorld, SurveyCompare) and complete your profiles fully. You can earn R500-800 in your first month with surveys alone. For longer-term, start freelancing or tutoring.

Can I work if I'm on NSFAS?

Generally yes, but there may be limits. Most universities allow students to earn up to R2,500 per month without affecting NSFAS. However, rules can change. Check with your university's financial aid office before starting work.

Earning money as a student in South Africa is not only possible – it's becoming common. With the right approach, you can gain financial independence, build your CV, and still succeed academically.
