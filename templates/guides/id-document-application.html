How to Apply for a Smart ID Card

Complete guide to getting your South African Smart ID Card - first-time applications, reissues, and replacements

6 min read Updated 2026
Nompumelelo Sibiya March 2026 Home Affairs Guide
Smart ID Card vs Green ID Book: The Smart ID Card is the new standard. It's more secure, durable, and easier to carry. Green ID books are still valid but are being phased out.

Who Can Apply for a Smart ID?

  • First-time applicants: All South Africans turning 16 years and older
  • Replacing lost/stolen ID: Report to SAPS first, get an affidavit
  • Upgrading from green ID book: Voluntary replacement
  • Name or status changes: Marriage, divorce, or naturalization

Required Documents

First-time application (16+):

  • Birth certificate (original or certified copy)
  • Parent/guardian ID (if under 18)
  • Proof of residence (municipal bill or affidavit)

Replacement (lost/stolen):

  • Police affidavit reporting the loss
  • Any form of identification (if available)
  • Proof of residence

Upgrade from green ID book:

  • Current green ID book
  • Proof of residence

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Book an Appointment (Optional but Recommended)
Visit services.dha.gov.za, click "Book Appointment", select your nearest Home Affairs office, choose a date and time, save your appointment reference.
Step 2: Visit Home Affairs Office
Arrive early (before 7:30 AM if no appointment), bring all required documents, take a number, present documents to the officer.
Step 3: Complete Application Form
Fill in the DHA-9 form (provided at the office), provide accurate personal information, verify spelling matches your documents.
Step 4: Biometric Capture
Have your photo taken (dress neatly, no hats), provide fingerprints (all 10 fingers), sign on the digital tablet.
Step 5: Pay the Fee
First-time application: Free (at 16 years), Replacement/Upgrade: R140, Lost/Stolen: R140 + police affidavit. Pay at the office cashier.
Step 6: Collect Your Smart ID
Processing time: 7-14 working days, you'll receive an SMS when ready, bring your receipt and ID book (if any) to collect, collection must be done in person.

Smart ID Costs 2026

  • First-time at 16: Free
  • Replacement (damaged): R140
  • Lost/Stolen: R140 + police affidavit fee
  • Upgrade from green ID: R140
  • Correction of details: Free (if DHA error)
Processing Times:
Standard: 7-14 working days, Peak periods (December-January): 3-4 weeks, SMS notification when ready for collection.

Banking Hours vs Home Affairs

Some banks offer Home Affairs services at selected branches. FNB, Standard Bank, Absa, Nedbank have eHome Affairs services. Online application: upload documents, then visit bank for biometrics. Faster service with often shorter queues than Home Affairs offices.

Bank eHome Affairs Process:
Apply online at ehome.dha.gov.za, upload required documents, book appointment at participating bank branch, visit bank for biometric capture, collect Smart ID at same bank branch (7-14 days).

What to Do If Your ID Is Lost or Stolen

  1. Report to nearest SAPS station immediately
  2. Obtain an affidavit (case number)
  3. Take affidavit and any other ID to Home Affairs
  4. Apply for a replacement Smart ID
  5. Pay the R140 fee
  6. Wait 7-14 days for processing
Pro Tips:
Apply at least 3 months before your 16th birthday, book online to avoid long queues, bring your own pen and extra copies of documents, arrive 30 minutes before opening time.
Security Note:
Never leave your ID with anyone, report lost/stolen immediately to prevent fraud, keep a certified copy at home, Smart ID cards should not be laminated.
Contact Information:
DHA Call Centre: 0800 60 11 90 (toll-free)
Website: www.dha.gov.za
eHome Affairs: ehome.dha.gov.za
