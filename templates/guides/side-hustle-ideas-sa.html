20+ Side Hustle Ideas in South Africa

Practical ways to earn extra income while working or studying - Updated for 2026

12 min read Updated 2026 Zero to low investment
Nompumelelo Sibiya March 2026 Money Guide
Quick Overview: These side hustles range from online gigs to local services. Most require only a smartphone and internet connection to get started.

📱 Online Side Hustles (Work from Home)

1. Freelance Writing & Content Creation

Write articles, blog posts, or social media content for businesses. Platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, and local Facebook groups have constant demand.

Potential: R200-R1,500 per article
2. Online Surveys & Micro-tasks

Join platforms like SurveySA, MOBROG, and Clickworker to earn by completing surveys, testing apps, or doing small tasks.

Potential: R500-R2,000/month
3. Online Tutoring

Tutor school subjects, English, or specialized skills via Zoom. Platforms like TutorMe, or advertise locally on Facebook groups.

Potential: R150-R400/hour
4. Dropshipping / Print-on-Demand

Sell products online without holding inventory. Use Shopify or takealot.com to start your e-commerce business.

Potential: R1,000-R10,000+/month
5. Virtual Assistant

Help businesses with emails, scheduling, social media, and admin tasks. Many international clients hire South African VAs.

Potential: R3,000-R8,000/month
6. Social Media Management

Manage social media accounts for small businesses. Create content, schedule posts, and engage with followers.

Potential: R1,500-R5,000/client/month

🚗 Local & Service-Based Side Hustles

7. Home Cooking / Meal Prep Business

Cook and sell meals to neighbors, students, or working professionals. Start small and grow through word of mouth.

Potential: R500-R3,000/week
8. Delivery Services

Sign up for Uber Eats, Mr D, or Bolt Food. Use a bicycle or car to deliver food in your area.

Potential: R200-R500/day
9. Hair & Beauty Services

Offer braiding, makeup, nail art, or barber services from home or mobile visits.

Potential: R150-R500/service
10. Handyman / Cleaning Services

Offer gardening, car washing, deep cleaning, or small repairs in your neighborhood.

Potential: R300-R800/day
11. Craft & Handmade Goods

Sell handmade items like jewelry, candles, soaps, or traditional crafts at markets or on Facebook Marketplace.

Potential: R500-R3,000/month
12. Pet Sitting / Dog Walking

Offer to walk dogs or pet sit while owners are at work or on holiday.

Potential: R100-R300/day

💻 Tech & Digital Skills Side Hustles

13. Web Design & Development

Build simple websites for small businesses using WordPress or Wix. No coding experience needed for basic sites.

Potential: R2,000-R8,000/project
14. Data Entry & Transcription

Enter data or transcribe audio files for companies. Platforms like Rev, TranscribeMe, or local listings.

Potential: R50-R200/hour
15. Photography & Videography

Offer photography for events, portraits, or product photos. Edit videos for content creators.

Potential: R500-R3,000/shoot
16. Translation Services

If you speak multiple languages (isiZulu, Afrikaans, etc.), offer translation services online.

Potential: R200-R500/page

📦 Reselling & Trading

17. Clothing Reselling

Buy second-hand clothes from thrift stores and resell on Facebook Marketplace or Instagram.

Potential: R100-R500/item
18. Phone & Gadget Refurbishing

Buy used phones, repair minor issues, and resell at a profit. Learn from YouTube tutorials.

Potential: R500-R2,000/device
19. Baking & Catering

Bake cakes, cupcakes, or bread for birthdays and events. Build a following on Instagram.

Potential: R200-R1,500/order

📝 How to Get Started Today

Step 1: Pick ONE hustle - Don't try to do everything at once. Choose what matches your skills and available time.

Step 2: Start small - Test the market before investing heavily. Use free platforms first.

Step 3: Promote yourself - Create a simple Facebook/Instagram page. Ask friends and family for referrals.

Step 4: Be consistent - Success takes time. Keep showing up and improving your service.

Step 5: Scale up - Once you have regular clients, consider reinvesting profits to grow.
Important Tips:
• Avoid "get rich quick" schemes - legitimate side hustles take effort
• Never pay money to start a job - real opportunities don't ask for upfront payment
• Keep track of your income for tax purposes
• Start with what you have - don't borrow money to start
Useful Resources:
• Upwork / Fiverr - Freelance platforms
• Facebook Marketplace - Sell products locally
• Canva - Free design tool for marketing materials
• WhatsApp Business - Manage customers easily
Quick Start Action Plan:
Week 1: Choose your side hustle and set up social media page
Week 2: Tell friends/family and get first 3 customers
Week 3: Collect testimonials and ask for referrals
Week 4: Scale and add another service if needed
